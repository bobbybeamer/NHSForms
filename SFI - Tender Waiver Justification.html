Standing Financial Instructions Competitive Tender Waiver Justification Form

Use this form to document the rationale, policy basis, and funding details for an exception to formal tendering procedures.

SFIW00001 Form number
CP105 Policy reference

Important guidance

Approval to waive the Trust Standing Financial Instructions for formal tendering procedures by the Chief Executive cannot be considered a foregone conclusion.

Only in very exceptional circumstances can the Chief Executive consider a request to waive the Trust’s Standing Financial Instructions for formal tendering procedures. Any such request must fall within the remit of Section D Point 9.4 of Trust Policy CP105 and only applies where the contract does not exceed the Public Contracts Regulations supplies and services threshold of £138,760 including VAT.

Completed forms must not be submitted directly to the Chief Executive or Chief Finance Officer. Return completed forms to the Procurement Team at HRI. If you wish to discuss this further, contact the procurement team before submission.

Section 2

Waiver justification

The waiving of competitive tendering procedures should not be used to avoid competition, for administrative convenience, or to award further business to a consultant or supplier originally appointed through a competitive procedure.

Section 3

Reason for waiver request

Select the applicable policy basis below. These justifications are detailed in Trust Policy 105: Standing Orders, Reservation and Delegation of Powers and Standing Financial Instruction.

Option Select Description
A In very exceptional circumstances where the Chief Executive or Chief Financial Officer decides that formal tendering procedures would not be practicable or the estimated expenditure or income would not warrant formal tendering procedures, and the circumstances are detailed in an appropriate Trust record, excluding tenders over the FTS limit.
B Where the requirement is covered by an existing contract.
C Where a consortium arrangement is in place and a lead organisation has been appointed to carry out tendering activity on behalf of the consortium members.
D Where the timescale genuinely precludes competitive tendering but failure to plan the work properly would not be regarded as a justification for a single tender. This only applies where the value of the contract does not exceed the FTS limit.
E Where specialist expertise is required and is available from only one source. This only applies where the value of the contract does not exceed the FTS limit.
F When the task is essential to complete the project, and arises as a consequence of a recently completed assignment and engaging different consultants for the new task would be inappropriate. This only applies where the value of the contract does not exceed the FTS limit.
G Where there is a clear benefit to be gained from maintaining continuity with an earlier project and the benefits of continuity outweigh any potential financial advantage from competitive tendering. This only applies where the value of the contract does not exceed the FTS limit.
H For the provision of legal advice and services where the legal firm or partnership is regulated by the Law Society for England and Wales, or by the Bar Council for England and Wales for Counsel’s opinion, and is recognised as having sufficient expertise in the relevant area of work.
